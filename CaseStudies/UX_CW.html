ChaseWick.com

Research
We started by scouting out the competitors, evaluating their strengths and weaknesses.

Our competitor analysis was utilized with direct and indirect competitors. For direct competitors, we evaluated Yankee Candle and Bowes Signature Candles.

For indirect competitors, we evaluated Bed, Bath and Beyond, Target, and Sweet Water Decor. Looking at the strengths and weaknesses of these two different types of competitors helped us gain knowledge on what our customers might or might not want.

Proto Persona
We wanted a user who had a wide variety of wants, needs.. What he will do or not do when it comes to buying candles online. Some of Chase's wants were monthly subscription options, pre selecting candles, easy payment and cancelation options, shipping updates and knowing some of money towards the purchase went to charity.
User interviews give us better understanding when it comes to consumers.
We conducted a total of 4 user interviews to see what people like when it comes to candles and how they prefer to purchase them. These interviews helped us learn more about what people want when it comes to price, scent types, how long they last, if they would prefer a monthly subscription or not, if they care about a percentage of the price going to charity or if they would be interested in recycling or not.
Definition
Affinity diagram was divided into three sections:
Consisting of the reasons our customers would buy our product, what kind of description they prefer them to have, and if they would just buy them once or prefer a monthly subscription. This data collected allowed us to analyze how to set up or site even more.
Empathy Map
allowed us to evaluate what our customers might do, say, think or feel about our website and product. Under the “feel” section, we were able to see the Pains and Gains of our possible future customers.
After gaining sufficient insights, we compiled our User Persona.
Candles are a unique item that everyone pretty much loves and buys. We wanted a user persona that fits right in the middle demographic of a candle buyer. John Doe is a single, 27 male that works at Starbucks. He buys candles for personal use when he wants his place to smell good but sometimes as he buys them as gifts. He typically buys them at retail stores while shopping for other items.
User Insight

"During the interviews, we discovered that scent, description of candle, and price are most important.

Therefore, we believe that candle shopping website would help the consumers in picking the perfect gifts or personal use items practically.

We might do this by offering descriptive details, comparisons between different products, subscription service, and gift wrapping. Doing this will allow our product to reach the needs of people seeking to buy candles for gift purposes and/or personal use."

Problem Statement

"Our website was designed to achieve an all-in-one shopping experience for candles. We have observed that local retailers do not provide all the services that our website could and with limited options.

How might we improve our website so that our customers are more willing to shop online?"

Ideation

Reflecting our problem statement, we set up a scenario where our user John Doe is trying to pick up some candles for himself and his mother.

He starts by shopping at a Target, but fails to find a candle that fits his criteria. After receiving a recommendation to check out ChaseWick.com, John finds a wide variety of candles, and some interesting options to have them gift-wrapped. This allows him to find just the right candle for his mother and have it look nice as he presents it to her.

User Journey Map

We also studied the process of purchasing a candle in a storefront, as physical candle stores will remain a major competitor to our site, seeing as you cannot smell a candle through a computer screen.

We posited that the process of perusing and purchasing candles is largely a positive experience, although the usage of the candle is most likely the best part. Our goal with Chasewick was to try and make a shopping experience that is equally pleasant.

Site Map

Our focus on the shopping process went into our development of the site map, where we planned our prototype.

Largely we focused on what would be needed on every page, which is the navbar and footer. Knowing this would be used as the foundation of our prototype, we condensed our points in the bottom right corner into two sections: features available and pages required.

User Flow

Namely, our user flow, which depicts how somebody such as our John Doe may go through the website. Starting from the home page, looking through some specifically pointed-out candles, reading through multiple product pages, and then finally going through the process of buying one of the candles.

  1. Starts at Homepage
  2. Browses the carousel
  3. Browses through current promotions
  4. Picks a random product
  5. Reads through the description
  6. Picks 2 more candles
  7. Compares the 3 choices
  8. Adds the one he likes most to cart
  9. Goes to checkout
  10. Adds gift-wrap options
  11. Finalizes the purchase
Prototyping
Moodboard

The first thing we did before sketching was make a moodboard. We chose sites such as Target, Coastline Soap, and IKEA for useful points of inspiration. We also picked a few images for a general mood to the site as well as a potential color scheme.

Lo-fidelity prototype

The most important step during the prototyping phase. At this point we visualized how the site would look and how we would execute all the researches into the layout of the website.

We started with the Mobile version first due to the real estate constrain. If we could translate all the researches to fit the mobile screen, the Desktop version would become a fancy playground.

Conclusion
Team work and organization are keys.

With ChaseWick completed, we learned a great deal especially when it comes to E-commerce. Our site is running and responsive, which taught us a lot about how UI developers work and what they need from designers. It also opened our eyes to how to work as a team, not just designing, but working together with coding - and being careful not to override each others iterations to the site.

In the future we hope to add working videos, add more individual product pages, work on the Subscription Page, and continue improving the website.
