Overview
We redesigned the nonprofit organization's website called Arts for All to create a better experience for the users.

The Problem:

Future Arts for All volunteers would like to know in great detail what their job entails and what the commitment implies.


The Solution:

We believe we will help them maintaining an organized and updated list of positions available and detailing all the necessary information. Doing this will allow our product to benefit the user by providing a smooth process and help the community by enabling more people to offer a helping hand to this nonprofit.

Research
In the research process, each team member conducted at least one interview with potential user to get the first insights.

Through the 5 interviews, we could understand their behaviors, opinions, past experiences, and suggestions for potential organizations. We prepared questions strategically divided into warm up questions, past experiences, and suggestions for organizations.

We got a lot of important insights from our interviews that helped us to understand how the users behave.

Ideation

With all the insights that we got on the interviews we created the Affinity Diagram to organize related facts and insights. We could see some patterns on this research, so we divided the diagram into reasons, pain points, research, and experiences.

After building the empathy map where we could visualize volunteer attitudes, behaviors, and frustrations that helped us to align on a deeper understanding. So we could see that they research about the organization before they volunteer, likes to have detailed information and schedule about the position, and understand how they can help the community.

Next step, we came up with our proto-persona, Diana Andrea. She is 24 years old, lives in Manhattan NY, and works as substitute teacher, and a freelance artist. She is always looking for opportunities to volunteer, and her goal is to help the community while improving her communication and networking skills.

Evaluation
To begin our design we had to pinpoint the issues from the current site that we wanted to focus on by conducting a heuristic evaluation.

We pin-point few of the problems as priorities.

For example, there were too many categories in the navigation bar, which made it confusing for users to find anything in specific, and a little overwhelming if they just wanted to browse around.

Secondly, we had a lot of contents, which we decided to condense with the same goal of making it less overwhelming for the user.

Afterwards, we compiled a value proposition canvas.

The Value Proposition Canvas helped us to make sure that the product we are designing is positioned around volunteers' values and needs.

Final Thoughts

In this project, we realized the importance of the UI Style Guide is in a design project and how it could be a challenge when you are redesigning a website with two very bold colors in its theme. We decided to keep the same color theme as the original website, so we had to find strategies to make the combination looks more professional and concise.

When we were working on our Responsive Web Design, we had to find creative ways to work with the content and organization to be able to communicate the same information similarly but on a completely different size screen.
