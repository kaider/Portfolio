Virtual Teaching Platform

The all-in-one virtual teaching platform for teachers.

Virtual teachers have a lot on their plates and have to use multiple
resources to plan and teach. With ULearn, teachers have a convienent
all-in-one place to help them with all their classroom needs.

Research

We started by creating a proto-persona to help us determine interview questions and our target audience.

Once we completed 6 interviews with teachers and transcribed them, we completed an affinity diagram using Figjam to help visualize key things that stand out, such as the amount of resources that teachers currently use to plan lessons.

Then, we created an empathy map to help further understand our users key pains and gains and further develop our teacher user-persona.

The 8 key problems/concerns:

  • Enjoyments about Virtual Teaching
  • Troubles with Attendance
  • Difficult Student Engagement
  • Parent Involvement
  • Difficulty with Programs
  • Experiences about Teaching Virtually
  • Resources commonly used
  • Resources needed
    •

    Definition

    Using our research we were able to identify the main problems that teachers face.

    As a team we then decided that our solution for this was to create an all-in-one virtual teaching platform that teachers could use to prepare lessons in a quick and seamless way.

    We then decided to complete a competitor analysis of similar websites, such as Canvas, Nearpod, Zoom, and Microsoft Teams... that allowed us to discuss their features, strengths, and weaknesses and use that information to guide our ideation process.

    Ideation

    Using the "I like, I wish, What If" method, we were able to generate ideas that could be used within the app.

    Using those ideas we created a feature priotization matrix, to see which of them were integal to our creation - such as having a gradebook and creating lessons available right on the app.

    Our value proposition canvas allowed us to identify how those features would help users current pains with preparing lessons and what users would gain from using our app.

    We then created a storyboard, with a drawn out idea of how our users would find value in our app after dealing with the frustrations of online teaching.

    Prototyping

    We started first with a mind-map to help our team visualize what would be covered in each area of our navigation, and how we would cohesively design it. We then created a Figma file that allowed us to build out wireframes of each section and put in the interactions that users would use in order to sign into the app, create a lesson, and review the gradebook.
